Past Tense

Uses and Explanation

You need past tense for talking about anything that already happened. For past affirmative you take out the "ます" from a conjugated verb and add "ました". For the negative version you do the same but add "ませんでした" instead.

Examples

--ます-->--ました
ex: します-->しました　かえります-->かえりました

Short Form

Uses and Explanation

Short Form is used in informal speech, which is used to indicate closeness/intimacy. It is only used between peers, friends and those younger to you and never to seniors. It is also used to quote other people.

Present affirmative form: Short Form=dictionary form
Present negative form:
ru verbs: -ない　＋ない
u-verbs: -u +あない　Exceptions: -う　+わない and あるー－＞　いない
Irregular: する　ー－＞　しない　　くる　ー－＞　こない

Examples

Present affirmative form: かえるますー－＞　かえる
Present negative form:
ru verbs: かえるー－＞　かえない
u-verbs: きくー－＞　きかない
